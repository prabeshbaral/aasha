Our Solution: Biodegradable Plastic from Corn Starch

At Team AASHA, we address the plastic pollution crisis by introducing an innovative and sustainable solution: biodegradable plastic derived from corn starch.

Our solution leverages a renewable and cost-effective material—corn starch—to create plastics that retain the convenience of traditional plastics while being eco-friendly and biodegradable.

Key Features of Our Solution

  • Eco-Friendly: Made from renewable materials and biodegradable in nature.
  • Cost-Effective: Uses readily available ingredients like corn starch, distilled water, glycerol, and vinegar.
  • Scalable Process: Simple production techniques that can be easily scaled for industrial applications.
  • Multi-Purpose: Suitable for applications in retail, packaging, and more.

How It Works

The production process involves a few straightforward steps:

  1. Extract Corn Starch: Obtain corn starch from corn granules.
  2. Prepare Mixture: Mix corn starch, water, glycerol, and vinegar.
  3. Heat and Mold: Heat the mixture and shape it into desired forms.
  4. Cool and Dry: Let the product cool and dry, creating biodegradable plastic.

Why Choose Biodegradable Plastic?

By replacing conventional plastics with biodegradable alternatives, we can significantly reduce environmental harm, preserve marine ecosystems, and promote sustainable practices.
