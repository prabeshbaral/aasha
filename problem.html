The Problem: Plastic Pollution

Plastics are essential in modern life, but they come at a great cost to the environment. Approximately 98% of plastics are derived from fossil fuels, contributing significantly to greenhouse gas emissions. Globally, plastic production generates 1.96 gigatons of CO₂ annually.

Due to their non-biodegradable nature, plastics persist in the environment for up to 1,000 years, causing long-term ecological harm. Key consequences include:

  • Marine Life Destruction: Over 100,000 marine animals die annually due to plastic ingestion or entanglement.
  • Soil Degradation: Microplastics alter soil composition, reducing fertility and affecting agriculture.
  • Groundwater Contamination: Toxic chemicals leached from plastics pollute water supplies.

Awareness Without Action

Despite increased awareness of these environmental harms, a convenient, affordable, and sustainable alternative to conventional plastics is still unavailable in most markets. The lack of viable options leaves industries, businesses, and consumers relying heavily on single-use plastics.

At Team AASHA, we recognize the urgency of this challenge and are committed to developing innovative solutions to combat the plastic pollution crisis.
